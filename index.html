Graduation! ~ 24/07/2024

Today, I proudly graduate from the University of Nottingham with a degree in Natural Sciences, focusing on Physics and Earth Science. My time here has been more than just academic—it’s been a journey of personal growth, discovery, and perseverance. Reflecting on these past few years, I feel a mix of excitement, pride, and gratitude for the experiences and people that shaped my journey.
Choosing Natural Sciences stemmed from a curiosity about the fundamental laws of the universe and how our planet operates. Physics challenged me to think critically about space, time, and matter, while Earth Science grounded that knowledge in real-world issues like climate modeling and pollution.
The road wasn’t always easy. Balancing assignments, exams, and part-time work tested my resilience, but these challenges helped me grow. I’ll never forget the late nights revising for quantum mechanics or the demanding conservation project in Earth Science. These experiences taught me time management, persistence, and the value of asking for help.
Graduation is both an end and a beginning. Armed with the knowledge and skills I’ve gained, I’m excited for whatever comes next. The foundation I’ve built here will guide me in the future.

Glastonbury with WaterAid

Volunteering for WaterAid at Glastonbury was an unforgettable experience. Being part of such a huge festival while supporting a cause I truly care about was incredibly rewarding. We spent our days helping raise awareness about global water issues, ensuring festival-goers had access to clean water, sanitation and promoting sustainability. It was amazing to see how a small effort in such a vibrant setting could make a big impact.
Beyond the music and the fun, the highlight for me was knowing that our work was contributing to lasting change in communities worldwide. I am deeply grateful to have been part of a team so committed to making a difference. It wasn’t just a festival; it was an opportunity to give back and help bring clean water to those who need it most. Thank you, WaterAid, for this life-changing experience! See you next year!

Sweden

This summer, I had the incredible opportunity to attend a month-long summer school program at Linköping University in Sweden, focused on Nanotechnology: Sustainability Challenges and Social Impact. The program was a deep dive into the fascinating world of nanotechnology, exploring how these tiny innovations are shaping big solutions to some of our most pressing global challenges.
Throughout the course, we learned about the potential of nanotechnology to revolutionize fields like energy, water purification, and medical diagnostics, but we also critically examined the environmental and ethical implications. The discussions on sustainability and how nanotech can be both a solution and a challenge were eye-opening.
Beyond the academics, living in Sweden for a month was a cultural experience in itself. The university’s welcoming environment, coupled with meeting students from around the world, made it an unforgettable experience. We not only shared knowledge, but also perspectives on how technology can shape a more sustainable future.
This summer school reinforced my passion for science and sustainability and gave me a new appreciation for the global impact of innovation. I’m incredibly grateful for the opportunity and excited to apply what I’ve learned moving forward.
